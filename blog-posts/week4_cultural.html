The filetype issue

I think a major issue facing the tech industry is the public's adoption rate of new technologies. People are so trained to use what is comfortable to them that even an update that brings functionality, style, load times, etc. is met with "WE HATE CHANGE" reactions. Some people hold their online identity very closely and it's understandable that once certain people develop an online comfort zone, that digital threats to that status quo are met with passionate objection (whether those threats are an improvement or not.) The best example of this is Facebook. Everytime facebook has tweaked its layout slightly, large amounts of my friends would moan and complain about how facebook is awful, how they're going to delete it, how they're so dumb for not listening to their userbase, etc.
Well, SHOULD you listen to your userbase if you know they will just end up adapting eventually to the newer, better layout? Or should you neglectfully let them wallow in rapidly outdating formats that they are more familiar with? It is frustrating to see people who are so afraid of change when it is almost always for the better.
